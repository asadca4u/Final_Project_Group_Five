Diabetes, a chronic metabolic disorder, is one of the fastest growing diseases regardless of geographic, racial, or ethnic context. There are two types of diabetes: Type I and Type II. - Type I diabetes happens when the pancreatic beta cells produce either very less insulin or no insulin. - Type II diabetes happens when body become insulin resistance. The common symptoms for Type I diabetes are sudden weight loss, Dehydration, delayed healing, blurred vision and Type II have similar symptoms or no symptoms at all depends on body reaction. [1, 2]. Type I diabetes strikes both kids and adults at any age. There is no cure for Diabetes, but it can be managed if it diagnoses in early stage. Type I diabetes can be managed by medication and Type II can be managed by balanced diet, exercise and a healthy lifestyle. In this project, we will be dealing exclusively with data about Type II diabetic patients.
